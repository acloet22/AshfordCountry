William Ashford


Born: August 20, 1900
Died: March 15, 1980

William Ashford was one of the most influential horror writers in the 20th century. He began writing at a very early age, many of his works are a mix of science fiction, gothic, and horror. He was born to Martha and Johnathan Ashford on August 20th 1900, in the city of Haven's Port. The pair were both well into their forties, there are some rumors that William may have been an accidental birth.

Early Success

Young William did modestly well in his studies during elementary. However he did struggle with subjects such as Math, however he excelled at English and Writing. After school William would usually head to the town's library where he eagerly read books more suited for older audiences, or create his own short stories. He would stay there until he was retrieved by either of his parents after they had got off work. Writing by candle and lantern light while working on his tales at his desk. By the age of nine William had begun to show signs of an unknown mental illness. His parents would find him wandering the halls late at night in a trance like state. He would utter random words in this state. (From the few recordings we posses it is believed this may have been a combination of his writing and dreams. The short Story the Chosen few was believe to have been based upon these events). While his father did not approve of his interest or talents his grandmother urged him to continue his work.

Love and Loss

William Ashford graduated a few years after his classmates. He was unable to attend any further schooling immediately after his graduation. Death

On March 15, 1980 William Ashford passed away peacefully in his sleep. He was found deceased by his friend Avel. He was given a private funeral with the few friends and family that still remained. He was buried near his home with his wife in Saint Geneva Cemetery. However the horror community mourned his loss as well. It was said that author Regina Stevens held a vigal for the author with other colleagues.
